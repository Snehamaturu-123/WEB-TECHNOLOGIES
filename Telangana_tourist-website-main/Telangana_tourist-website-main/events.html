Bonalu

Bonalu is a Hindu festival that honors Goddess Mahakali and is celebrated annually in the twin cities of Hyderabad and Secunderabad, and other parts of Telangana, India. The festival is celebrated during Ashada Masam, which falls in July or August, and is known for its vibrant processions, traditional music, dance, and festive atmosphere.

Dussehra

The festival commemorates the victory of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana. On the day of Dussehra, sculptures of Ravana and his brothers Meghnad and Kumbhakarna are burnt in public squares. This symbolises the victory of good over evil and the destruction of evil forces.

Peerla Panduga

Peerla Panduga, also known as Muharram, is a festival celebrated by Muslims and Hindus in the state of Telangana, India. It is marked by processions, and takes place across Sufi shrines called Ashurkhana. The festival is an occasion of sorrow when Hindu devotees respect the sentiments of their Islamic brothers

Sammakka Saarakka Jaathara

Sammakka Sarakka Jathara, also known as Medaram Jathara, is a festival that honors the goddesses Sammakka and Saralamma, and their fight against an unjust law with the reigning rulers. The festival takes place in Medaram, a remote village in the Eturnagaram Wildlife Sanctuary, once every two years. The festival commemorates the mother-daughter duo's courage when they fought against a despotic regime that wanted to tax the people of Medaram during a drought

Teej Festival

Teej festival is celebrated by Banjara(also known as Lambadas) and Mathura communities in the old Adilabad district of Telangana. Amid displaced homes and constant moving, their life is an eternal search for small joys, which they find in festivities.
